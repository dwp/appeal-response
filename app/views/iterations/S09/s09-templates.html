Facts of the case - a template based approach

Hypothesis / Experiment

  • We have seen that
    - appeal responses do not meet DWP and HMCTS quality standards
    - the MS Word templates set out are either changed or not used at all
  • We think this is because
    - the templates are overwritten 
    - the appeal writers find a need to add more to the existing templates
    - the right part of the template is not edited or incorrectly edited
  • So if we
    - Guide AW's through a set of template selection
    - And ask the appeal writers to just fill in the missing information
  • We will see
    - increased uniformity, increased throughput and increased accuracy
  • We'll know this is true when
    - QA has a high assurance ratio/rating 
    - AW's have a reduced appeal handling time per response
    - a reduction in direction notices from HMCTS (including regs referred to) 

What's new or changed?

  • In the previous iteration we tested a simpler set of questions, answers to which produced the facts of the case. In this iteration we are experimenting with replicating the behaviour of MS Word templates.
  • Ask the appeal writers to choose the relevant template and only ask them to fill in what is missing.

What did we learn?

  • There was a very positive response to the ‘facts of the case building’ functionality from appeal response writers.
  • Value in both time-saving and accuracy, and value over existing approaches / templates. (All quotes below from UC appeal writers)​

Some quotes from the users

“I’d much rather use this and fill in key information, than having to sift through things I don’t need.”
“It would make writing of an appeal a lot faster and a consistent format. Which I think is a big thing.“ ​
“The concept is similar to what we’re doing now This is doing the same thing, but in a much clearer way."​

The positive reaction was sometimes qualified with considerations.

  • It's not this “cut and dried” for complex cases with multiple elements.
  • There may be multiple changes recorded – and this system doesn’t seem to account for that.
  • Question set wasn’t considered sufficiently complete by two participants – could envisage other scenarios the responses didn’t cover.
  • If dealing with an overpayment and entitlement decision, participant wouldn’t want to go in and make two decisions.

Next steps

  • Need to address the complex cases
  • The names of the templates are too similar and hence might lead to a wrong selection of template. Need to explore other ways to select the right template.
