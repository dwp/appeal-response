Question-led approach per appellant point

Hypothesis / Experiment

  • We have seen that appeal responses do not meet DWP and HMCTS quality standards
  • We think this is because
    We are asked for additional information from HMCTS 
    And PO's recognise variance in quality
  • So if we
    Guide AW's through a set of structured questions
    Answers to which automatically generate a response
  • We will see increased uniformity, increased throughput
  • We'll know this is true when
    QA has a high assurance ratio/rating 
    AW's have a reduced appeal handling time per response
    a reduction in direction notices from HMCTS (including regs referred to) 

What's new or changed?

  • A series of questions that will gather the necessary decision to find the right template.
  • The answers to the questions will customise the template and generate the right response.

What did we learn?

  • Both participants felt this seemed prescriptive, and there’s a significant challenge in getting the right questions.
  • “I think I’d have it as point and allow us to respond from that. It would be lovely if it was all flowcharty, unfortunately it doesn’t work like that. “
  • Both participants raised question about how the appellant points would be identified in the first place.
  • Customers are often not explicit about points.
  • When supported by external representative, they may be waiting for DWP response first before raising all points.
  • “How is it going to know to split the ramblings?“
  • One participant highlighted that legislative side was dealt with before appellant points.
  • First cite relevant law before addressing points.
  • Both participants acknowledged the editable aspect of this concept.
  • “I think you’d still need to be changing a lot of information. There’s too many shades of grey. “

Next steps

  • Need to test the concept of questionnaire with a different logic, possibly less intelligent.
  • Possibly ask a set of questions that the appeal writer may ask themselves while editing / selecting a template.
