Grounds of appeal

Sprint 11

PrepareAnAppealResponse_v4.0

Grounds of appeal

  • A structured questions based approach to responding to the grounds of appeal
  • Allow appeal writers to gather all the grounds of appeal at one place and respond to each
  • For our MVP, "Grounds of Appeal" would be out of scope and hence we will not iterate on this further as of now.
  • There was a desire to group similar grounds and reply to the group rather than individual ground
  • It wasn't intuitive that the appeal writers could add multiple grounds relating to the same appeal
  • Appeal writers might not know all the legilations for the type-ahead to work
  • There might be more than one evidence supporting the response

Facts of the case - a template based approach

Sprint 09

PrepareAnAppealResponse_v3.0

Facts of the case

Seeing it as it builds

  • In the previous iteration we tested a simpler set of questions, answers to which produced the facts of the case. In this iteration we are exerimenting with replicating the behaviour of MS Word templates.
  • Ask the appeal writers to choose the relevant template and only ask them to fill in what is missing.
  • Need to address the complex cases
  • The names of the templates are too similar and hence might lead to a wrong selection of template. Need to explore other ways to select the right template.
  • There was a very positive response to the ‘facts of the case building’ functionality from appeal response writers.
  • Value in both time-saving and accuracy, and value over existing approaches / templates. (All quotes below from UC appeal writers)​

Auto populate data from the evidence bundle

Sprint 09

PrepareAnAppealResponse_v3.0

Auto-population

  • In the previous iteration we tested auto populating date from the CCD system, in this version we want to experiment extracting that data from the evidence bundle.
  • We need to explore getting this data from a different source
  • Approaches to researching, building an evidence bundle and writing the response differ between appeal writers. We can’t assume that an evidence bundle will always be available, in a finalised form, at the point an appeal writer starts producing their response. ​

A new home

Sprint 09

PrepareAnAppealResponse_v3.0

Orientation

  • In the previous iteration the home page consisted of a list of previously created, this led to a few challenges with orientation of what this service was meant for. Some users thought, this will help them with their workflow. Hence in this iteration we simplified the homepage and created a new search page.
  • Since the evidence bundle might not have been created at the point of creating a response, we will have to extract that information from a different system.
  • We will continue evolving this simpler version of the home page and search page but might revert back to creating the response by extrating data from HMCTS' CCD system.
  • We should also explore if we can incorporate create and find in a single screen rather than separate screens.
  • In the previous sprint we mentioned ‘orientation’ issues relating to the ‘Appeal Response’ screens. These were less evident in this round of testing. Possible reasons (i) prior knowledge of prototype (ii) new “upload evidence” concept.​
  • There were ‘orientation’ findings relating to first screen users saw – including perception that it was the start of building the evidence bundle, or the first step in creating an appeal response, or compiling a response but with some form of response already in place.​

A simpler question set

Sprint 08

PrepareAnAppealResponse_v2.0

Facts of the case

  • In the previous iteration we tested a smart questionnaire that took the conditions of the appellant as an input and intelligently generated a response. However, this can be challenging to implement. This set of questionnaire is designed to ask the questions an Appeal Writer would ask themselves while choosing and editing a template.
  • We are fairly confident that a questions based approach to building the facts of the case is a viable and can lead to higher accuracy. In the next iteration we want to test a different mechanism for selecting the right template, one that will also increase speed along with accuracy.
  • Very well received, fewer doubts expressed vs. appellant points.​
  • Recognised potential for time-saving, accuracy and quality.​

Question-led approach per appellant point

Sprint 07

PrepareAnAppealResponse_v1.0

Grounds of appeal

  • A structured question approach to responding to appellant points, there’s potential to make the process quicker for appeal response writers, as well as leading to more accurate responses.
  • Need to test the concept of questionnaire with a different logic, possibly less intelligent.
  • Possibly ask a set of questions that the appeal writer may ask themselves while editing / selecting a template.
  • Both participants felt this seemed prescriptive, and there’s a significant challenge in getting the right questions.
  • Both participants raised question about how the appellant points would be identified in the first place.
  • One participant highlighted that legislative side was dealt with before appellant points.
  • Both participants acknowledged the editable aspect of this concept.
