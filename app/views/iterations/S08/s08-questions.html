Question-led approach per appellant point

Hypothesis / Experiment

  • We have seen that appeal responses do not meet DWP and HMCTS quality standards
  • We think this is because
    We are asked for additional information from HMCTS 
    And PO's recognise variance in quality
  • So if we
    Guide AW's through a set of structured questions
    Answers to which automatically generate a response
  • We will see increased uniformity, increased throughput
  • We'll know this is true when
    QA has a high assurance ratio/rating 
    AW's have a reduced appeal handling time per response
    a reduction in direction notices from HMCTS (including regs referred to) 

What's new or changed?

  • In the previous iteration we tested a smart questionnaire that took the conditions of the appellant as an input and intelligently generated a response. However, this can be challenging to implement. This set of questionnaire was designed to ask the questions an Appeal Writer would ask themselves while choosing and editing a template.
  • The answers to the questions will customise the template and generate the right response.

What did we learn?

  • Very well received, fewer doubts expressed vs. appellant points.
  • Recognised potential for time-saving, accuracy and quality.​

One of the participant said

Normally I’d be sitting with notebook and pen. I’m liking the chronological order and how it’s presenting.

Next steps

  • We are fairly confident that a questions based approach to building the facts of the case is a viable and can lead to higher accuracy. In the next iteration we want to test a different mechanism for selecting the right template, one that will also increase speed along with accuracy.
